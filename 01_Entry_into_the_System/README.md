# 01 - Entry into the System

## Purpose
This section covers the initial contact points between individuals and the criminal justice system, primarily through law enforcement interactions. It examines policies, procedures, technology, training, and accountability mechanisms.

## Contents

### Law Enforcement
- **Policies and Procedures**: Standard operating procedures, use of force policies, arrest protocols
- **Training and Certification**: Officer training programs, continuing education, specialized certifications
- **Technology and Surveillance**: Body cameras, predictive policing, AI risk detection, cybercrime tools
- **Accountability Mechanisms**: Internal affairs, civilian oversight, complaint procedures

## Key Topics

### Policing Models
- Community policing
- Problem-oriented policing
- Hot-spot policing
- Predictive policing and AI-assisted systems

### Technology Integration
- **Sentinel System**: AI-powered preemptive risk detection (see `Technology_and_Surveillance/`)
- Body-worn cameras and digital evidence
- License plate readers and surveillance systems
- Cybercrime investigation tools

### Accountability and Oversight
- Use of force review boards
- Civilian complaint processes
- Early warning systems for officer misconduct
- Transparency and public reporting

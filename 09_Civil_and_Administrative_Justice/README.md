## Key Topics

### Civil vs. Criminal Justice
Understanding the distinctions:
- **Burden of Proof**: Preponderance of evidence vs. beyond reasonable doubt
- **Parties**: Private parties vs. government prosecution
- **Remedies**: Monetary damages, injunctions vs. incarceration, fines
- **Rights**: No right to appointed counsel in most civil cases
- **Consequences**: Financial liability vs. loss of liberty

### Access to Justice
Civil justice gap for low-income individuals:
- **Legal Deserts**: Areas with few or no attorneys
- **Cost Barriers**: High attorney fees, court costs
- **Pro Se Representation**: Self-representation challenges
- **Legal Aid**: Insufficient funding for civil legal services
- **Technology Solutions**: Online dispute resolution, document automation

### Defamation and Harassment
Civil remedies for reputation harm:
- **Defamation**: False statements causing reputational harm
- **Libel**: Written defamation
- **Slander**: Spoken defamation
- **Online Harassment**: Cyberbullying, doxxing, impersonation
- **Restraining Orders**: Civil protection orders

### Digital Rights and Identity
Emerging civil law issues:
- **Identity Theft**: Civil remedies for identity theft victims
- **Deepfakes**: Unauthorized use of likeness
- **NCII**: Non-consensual intimate imagery
- **Data Breaches**: Privacy violations and data security
- **Platform Liability**: Section 230 and content moderation

## Key Issues and Reforms

### Access to Justice Gap
- **Problem**: 80% of civil legal needs of low-income Americans unmet
- **Solutions**:
  - Increased legal aid funding
  - Pro bono expansion
  - Limited scope representation
  - Technology solutions (ODR, document automation)
  - Simplified procedures

### Digital Rights Protection
- **Problem**: Inadequate legal remedies for digital harms
- **Solutions**:
  - Take It Down Act enforcement
  - Platform liability reform
  - Digital identity protection laws
  - Deepfake legislation
  - Cyberbullying statutes

### Family Court Reform
- **Problem**: Adversarial process harmful to families
- **Solutions**:
  - Collaborative divorce
  - Mediation and arbitration
  - Unified family courts
  - Trauma-informed practices
  - Child-centered approaches

### Administrative Justice
- **Problem**: Complex procedures, limited representation
- **Solutions**:
  - Simplified procedures
  - Right to counsel expansion
  - Online hearings
  - Plain language notices
  - Decision-making transparency

## Key Topics

### Problem-Solving Courts
Specialized dockets addressing root causes:
- **Drug Courts**: Treatment, testing, supervision, graduated sanctions/incentives
- **Mental Health Courts**: Treatment plans, case management, reduced incarceration
- **Veterans Courts**: VA services, peer mentors, understanding military culture
- **DUI Courts**: Intensive supervision, treatment, monitoring
- **Domestic Violence Courts**: Victim safety, offender accountability, coordinated response
- **Reentry Courts**: Post-release supervision and support

### Drug Treatment Courts
Most common problem-solving court model:
- **Structure**: Intensive supervision, frequent court appearances, drug testing
- **Treatment**: Evidence-based substance use treatment
- **Incentives**: Rewards for compliance (reduced supervision, graduation)
- **Sanctions**: Graduated responses to violations (increased supervision, brief jail)
- **Team Approach**: Judge, prosecutor, defense, treatment, probation
- **Effectiveness**: 35-80% reduction in recidivism (varies by study)

### Restorative Justice
Victim-offender dialogue and repair:
- **Principles**: Accountability, repair harm, reintegration
- **Practices**: Victim-offender mediation, family group conferencing, circles
- **Participants**: Victim, offender, community members, facilitator
- **Outcomes**: Restitution agreements, apologies, community service
- **Benefits**: Higher victim satisfaction, reduced recidivism, community healing

### Therapeutic Jurisprudence
Law as therapeutic agent:
- **Concept**: Legal processes can have therapeutic or anti-therapeutic effects
- **Application**: Design procedures to promote psychological well-being
- **Examples**: Problem-solving courts, trauma-informed practices
- **Considerations**: Balancing therapeutic goals with due process, autonomy

## Key Issues and Reforms

### Problem-Solving Court Expansion
- **Problem**: Limited availability, eligibility restrictions
- **Solutions**:
  - Expand to more jurisdictions
  - Broaden eligibility criteria
  - Increase funding and resources
  - Improve training and technical assistance
  - Enhance evaluation and quality assurance

### Coercion Concerns
- **Problem**: Pressure to participate, waiver of rights
- **Solutions**:
  - Voluntary participation
  - Informed consent
  - Right to counsel
  - Due process protections
  - Alternative options

### Effectiveness and Evaluation
- **Problem**: Mixed evidence on effectiveness
- **Solutions**:
  - Rigorous evaluation studies
  - Fidelity to evidence-based practices
  - Outcome measurement
  - Cost-benefit analysis
  - Continuous quality improvement

### Restorative Justice Integration
- **Problem**: Limited use in serious cases
- **Solutions**:
  - Expand to violent crimes
  - Integrate with traditional justice
  - Training for justice professionals
  - Victim choice and safety
  - Community capacity building

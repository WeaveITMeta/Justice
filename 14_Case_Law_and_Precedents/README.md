## Key Topics

### Constitutional Criminal Procedure

#### Fourth Amendment (Search and Seizure)
- **Mapp v. Ohio** (1961): Exclusionary rule applies to states
- **Terry v. Ohio** (1968): Stop and frisk standard
- **Katz v. United States** (1967): Reasonable expectation of privacy
- **United States v. Jones** (2012): GPS tracking is a search
- **Carpenter v. United States** (2018): Cell site location data requires warrant
- **Riley v. California** (2014): Cell phone search requires warrant

#### Fifth Amendment (Self-Incrimination)
- **Miranda v. Arizona** (1966): Right to remain silent, right to counsel
- **Dickerson v. United States** (2000): Miranda is constitutional rule
- **Berghuis v. Thompkins** (2010): Must explicitly invoke Miranda
- **Salinas v. Texas** (2013): Silence can be used against defendant if not invoked

#### Sixth Amendment (Right to Counsel, Fair Trial)
- **Gideon v. Wainwright** (1963): Right to appointed counsel in felonies
- **Argersinger v. Hamlin** (1972): Right to counsel for any jail time
- **Strickland v. Washington** (1984): Ineffective assistance of counsel standard
- **Batson v. Kentucky** (1986): Peremptory challenges cannot be racially motivated
- **Crawford v. Washington** (2004): Confrontation Clause and testimonial statements

#### Eighth Amendment (Cruel and Unusual Punishment)
- **Furman v. Georgia** (1972): Death penalty as applied is unconstitutional
- **Gregg v. Georgia** (1976): Death penalty constitutional with guidelines
- **Atkins v. Virginia** (2002): Cannot execute intellectually disabled
- **Roper v. Simmons** (2005): Cannot execute juveniles
- **Graham v. Florida** (2010): No life without parole for juvenile non-homicide
- **Miller v. Alabama** (2012): Mandatory life without parole for juveniles unconstitutional

### Sentencing and Punishment

#### Sentencing Guidelines
- **United States v. Booker** (2005): Federal guidelines advisory, not mandatory
- **Gall v. United States** (2007): Reasonableness standard for sentencing
- **Kimbrough v. United States** (2007): Can vary from guidelines for crack/powder disparity

#### Proportionality
- **Solem v. Helm** (1983): Proportionality review for non-capital sentences
- **Harmelin v. Michigan** (1991): Life without parole for drug possession upheld
- **Ewing v. California** (2003): Three strikes law upheld

### Civil Rights and Discrimination

#### Equal Protection
- **Brown v. Board of Education** (1954): Separate is not equal
- **Loving v. Virginia** (1967): Interracial marriage bans unconstitutional
- **McCleskey v. Kemp** (1987): Statistical evidence of racial disparity insufficient

#### Police Accountability
- **Tennessee v. Garner** (1985): Deadly force standard
- **Graham v. Connor** (1989): Objective reasonableness for use of force
- **Monell v. Department of Social Services** (1978): Municipal liability for civil rights violations
- **Qualified Immunity**: Protects officers unless rights clearly established

### Digital Privacy and Technology

#### Electronic Surveillance
- **Katz v. United States** (1967): Fourth Amendment protects people, not places
- **Smith v. Maryland** (1979): No expectation of privacy in phone numbers dialed (third-party doctrine)
- **Kyllo v. United States** (2001): Thermal imaging of home is a search
- **United States v. Jones** (2012): GPS tracking is a search
- **Riley v. California** (2014): Cell phone search requires warrant
- **Carpenter v. United States** (2018): Cell site location data requires warrant

#### Digital Evidence
- **United States v. Warshak** (2010): Email protected by Fourth Amendment
- **In re Search Warrant** (2016): Compelled decryption and Fifth Amendment
- **Blockchain Evidence**: Emerging case law on admissibility and authentication

#### Platform Liability
- **Section 230**: Immunity for user-generated content
- **Zeran v. America Online** (1997): Broad Section 230 immunity
- **Fair Housing Council v. Roommates.com** (2008): Limits to Section 230
- **Emerging Issues**: Take It Down Act, deepfakes, NCII

### Victim Rights

#### Constitutional Rights
- **Payne v. Tennessee** (1991): Victim impact statements constitutional
- **Crime Victims' Rights Act** (2004): Federal victim rights
- **State Constitutional Amendments**: Marsy's Law and similar provisions

#### Digital Rights
- **Take It Down Act** (2025): NCII removal requirements
- **Deepfake Cases**: Emerging litigation on unauthorized likeness use
- **Identity Theft**: Civil and criminal remedies

## Key Issues and Reforms

### Qualified Immunity Reform
- **Problem**: Shields officers from accountability
- **Solutions**:
  - Legislative elimination or limitation
  - Judicial reconsideration
  - Clearly established rights expansion
  - Municipal liability enhancement

### Sentencing Precedents
- **Problem**: Outdated precedents support harsh sentences
- **Solutions**:
  - Supreme Court review of proportionality
  - Retroactive application of new rules
  - Sentencing guideline revisions
  - Mandatory minimum challenges

### Digital Privacy Evolution
- **Problem**: Fourth Amendment not keeping pace with technology
- **Solutions**:
  - Carpenter expansion to other data
  - Third-party doctrine reconsideration
  - Warrant requirements for digital searches
  - Privacy legislation (not just case law)

### Victim Rights Enforcement
- **Problem**: Victim rights often not enforced
- **Solutions**:
  - Standing to enforce rights
  - Remedies for violations
  - Appellate review
  - Constitutional amendments

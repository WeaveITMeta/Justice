## Key Legislation

### Take It Down Act (2025)
**Purpose**: Mandatory platform compliance for non-consensual intimate imagery (NCII) removal and digital identity protection

**Key Provisions**:
- 48-hour takedown requirement for NCII
- Platform registration with federal authority
- Mandatory API integration for identity verification
- $10M+ penalties for non-compliance
- Government monitoring dashboard
- Cross-border enforcement mechanisms

**Implementation Requirements**:
- Upload intercept hooks at platform level
- Real-time blockchain logging
- Biometric identity verification
- Automated compliance reporting
- Evidence preservation for legal proceedings

**Covered Platforms**:
- Social media (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, Twitter/X, etc.)
- Content platforms (YouTube, Snapchat, Discord)
- Cloud services and file sharing
- AI platforms and image generators
- Gaming and metaverse platforms

### Digital Identity Protection Act (Proposed)
**Purpose**: Comprehensive framework for protecting digital identity across platforms

**Key Components**:
- Government ID + biometric registration system
- Blockchain-based identity tracking (Cardano primary)
- Cross-platform monitoring and enforcement
- Instant takedown authority for identity owners
- Legal evidence generation and preservation
- International coordination protocols

**Technology Requirements**:
- Multi-language SDK distribution (JS, Python, Rust, Java, Swift, Go, etc.)
- Platform-level integration (not browser extensions)
- Real-time identity scanning
- Perceptual hashing and biometric matching
- Zero-knowledge privacy proofs

## Legislative Framework

### Federal Enforcement Structure
1. **Platform Registration**: 30-day submission of integration plan
2. **Implementation Deadline**: 90-day mandatory compliance
3. **Monitoring**: Real-time government oversight dashboard
4. **Penalties**: Graduated enforcement ($10M first violation, $50M+ repeat)
5. **International Coordination**: Cross-border treaties and sanctions

### Constitutional Considerations
- **Fourth Amendment**: Privacy and search/seizure protections
- **First Amendment**: Free speech balancing with victim protection
- **Due Process**: Fair procedures and appeal mechanisms
- **Equal Protection**: Non-discriminatory application

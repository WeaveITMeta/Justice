# Cyber Crime

## What is Cyber Crime?
Cyber crime is a crime that is committed using the internet or other digital technologies. It can include a wide range of activities, such as identity theft, fraud, hacking, and cyberbullying.

## Types of Cyber Crime

1. Identity Theft
2. Fraud
3. Hacking
4. Cyberbullying
5. Cyberstalking
6. Cyber harassment
7. Cyber extortion
8. Cyber vandalism
9. Cyber espionage
10. Cyber terrorism

## How to Prevent Cyber Crime

1. Use strong passwords
2. Use antivirus software
3. Use a firewall
4. Use a VPN
5. Use a secure connection
6. Use a secure browser
7. Use a secure email
8. Use a secure messaging app
9. Use a secure payment method
10. Use a secure device

## How to Report Cyber Crime

1. Contact your local law enforcement agency
2. Contact your bank
3. Contact your credit card company
4. Contact your internet service provider
5. Contact your phone company
6. Contact your email provider
7. Contact your messaging app provider
8. Contact your payment processor
9. Contact your device manufacturer
10. Contact your antivirus software provider

## Deep Fakes
Deep fakes are videos that have been created using artificial intelligence to make it appear as if a person is saying or doing something that they have not said or done. They can be used for a variety of purposes, such as fraud, harassment, and blackmail.

## Targeting Bot Nets
Targeting bot nets are networks of computers that have been infected with malware and are being used to commit cyber crime. They can be used to launch distributed denial of service attacks, spread malware, and steal personal information.

## Illegal Scrapping
Illegal scrapping is the practice of scraping data from websites without permission. It can be used for a variety of purposes, such as identity theft, fraud, and cyberbullying.

## Social Engineering
Social engineering is the practice of using psychological manipulation to gain access to sensitive information. It can be used for a variety of purposes, such as identity theft, fraud, and cyberbullying.

## Public Friend Group Extortion
Public friend group extortion is the practice of extorting money from people by threatening to expose their personal information to their friends and family. It can be used for a variety of purposes, such as identity theft, fraud, and cyberbullying.

## Blackmail
Blackmail is the practice of extorting money from people by threatening to expose their personal information to their friends and family. It can be used for a variety of purposes, such as identity theft, fraud, and cyberbullying.

## Phishing
Phishing is the practice of using fake emails or websites to trick people into giving away sensitive information. It can be used for a variety of purposes, such as identity theft, fraud, and cyberbullying.

## Ransomware
Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts a person's files and demands a ransom in exchange for the decryption key. It can be used for a variety of purposes, such as identity theft, fraud, and cyberbullying.

## Cryptojacking
Cryptojacking is the practice of using a person's computer to mine cryptocurrency without their knowledge or consent. It can be used for a variety of purposes, such as identity theft, fraud, and cyberbullying.
